Frequently Asked Questions

What constitutes medical negligence in Seattle?
Medical negligence occurs when a healthcare professional fails to provide the standard of care that a reasonably competent doctor would have provided under similar circumstances, resulting in harm to the patient.
How can I prove that my doctors negligence caused my injury?
To prove medical negligence, you need to show that the doctor owed you a duty of care, breached this duty through their actions or omissions, directly caused your injury or harm, and resulted in specific damages such as medical bills, pain and suffering, or lost wages.
What is the statute of limitations for filing a medical malpractice lawsuit in Seattle?
In Washington state, you generally have three years from the date of the negligent act or one year from discovering the injury (whichever comes later), but no more than eight years after the act. Its crucial to consult with a lawyer promptly.
What kind of compensation can I expect from a successful medical malpractice claim?
You may be entitled to compensation for economic damages (like medical expenses and lost wages) and non-economic damages (such as pain and suffering). Punitive damages are rare but possible if gross negligence is proven.
How much does it cost to hire a medical malpractice lawyer in Seattle?
Most medical malpractice lawyers work on a contingency fee basis, meaning they only get paid if you win your case. The typical fee ranges from 25% to 40% of any settlement or judgment awarded.
