Frequently Asked Questions

What factors determine the settlement amount in a personal injury case?
The settlement amount is determined by several factors including the severity of injuries, medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, liability evidence, and insurance policy limits.
How long does it typically take to negotiate a settlement?
The time frame for negotiating a settlement can vary widely; it may take anywhere from a few weeks to several months depending on the complexity of the case and the willingness of both parties to reach an agreement.
Can I still go to court if Im not satisfied with the settlement offer?
Yes, if you are not satisfied with the settlement offer, you have the right to reject it and pursue litigation. However, this can be time-consuming and costly, so its important to weigh your options carefully with your attorney.
