Frequently Asked Questions

What is the first step in the litigation process for a personal injury case?
The first step is usually filing a complaint or petition with the court, which outlines your allegations and the basis for your claim against the defendant.
How long does it typically take to resolve a personal injury lawsuit?
The duration can vary widely depending on factors like case complexity, court schedules, and whether it goes to trial. Generally, it can take anywhere from several months to a few years.
Will I have to go to court if I file a personal injury lawsuit?
Not necessarily. Many personal injury cases are settled out of court through negotiations or mediation. However, if an agreement cannot be reached, going to trial may be necessary.
