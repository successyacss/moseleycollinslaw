What Is Medical Malpractice?

Medical malpractice occurs when a healthcare provider, such as a doctor, nurse, or hospital, fails to provide the standard of care expected in their profession, leading to patient harm. Common examples of medical malpractice include misdiagnosis or delayed diagnosis, surgical errors, medication mistakes, birth injuries, and anesthesia errors. Victims of medical malpractice may suffer from physical pain, emotional distress, additional medical expenses, and lost wages due to their injuries.

Why You Need a Medical Malpractice Lawyer

Medical malpractice cases are complex and challenging to prove. They require a thorough understanding of medical procedures, standards of care, and legal principles. An experienced medical malpractice lawyer can help gather evidence, consult with medical experts, and build a strong case to prove that a healthcare provider’s negligence caused harm.

In Washington State, there are specific laws and regulations governing medical malpractice claims, including a statute of limitations. Generally, a medical malpractice lawsuit must be filed within three years from the date of the injury or within one year of discovering the injury, whichever occurs first. However, there is also a maximum time limit of eight years from the date the malpractice occurred, regardless of when the injury was discovered. A knowledgeable lawyer will understand these legal requirements and ensure that the case is filed within the appropriate time frame.

Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifies as medical negligence in Washington?

Medical negligence occurs when a healthcare provider fails to meet the standard of care, resulting in harm or injury to a patient. This can include misdiagnosis, surgical errors, medication mistakes, or failure to provide appropriate treatment.

How long do I have to file a medical malpractice claim in Washington?

In Washington, you generally have three years from the date of the alleged negligence or one year from the date you discovered (or should have discovered) the injury, whichever is later. There are some exceptions and nuances, so consulting with an attorney promptly is crucial.

What damages can I recover in a medical malpractice case?

You can potentially recover economic damages (such as medical bills and lost wages), non-economic damages (like pain and suffering), and sometimes punitive damages if gross negligence is proven. Washington has caps on non-economic damages.

How do I prove that medical negligence occurred?

To prove medical negligence, you need to establish that there was a doctor-patient relationship, the healthcare provider breached the standard of care, this breach caused your injury, and you suffered actual damages as a result. Expert testimony is often required.

Do I need an attorney to pursue a medical malpractice claim?

While not legally required, having an experienced medical malpractice attorney is highly advisable due to the complexity of these cases. An attorney can help gather evidence, work with expert witnesses, navigate legal procedures, and negotiate settlements or represent you in court.
