Frequently Asked Questions

What is medical malpractice and how do I know if I have a case?
Medical malpractice occurs when a healthcare professional provides substandard care that results in harm to the patient. You may have a case if there was a breach of duty, causation, and damages. Consulting with an experienced attorney can help determine the validity of your claim.
How long do I have to file a medical malpractice claim in Seattle?
In Washington State, you generally have three years from the date of the alleged malpractice or one year from when you discovered (or reasonably should have discovered) the injury to file a claim. Consulting with an attorney promptly can help ensure you meet all deadlines.
What compensation might I be entitled to in a successful medical malpractice claim?
Compensation may include medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, loss of future earning capacity, and other related costs. An experienced attorney can provide a detailed assessment based on your specific circumstances.
How much does it cost to hire a medical malpractice lawyer in Seattle?
Many medical malpractice attorneys work on a contingency fee basis, meaning they only get paid if you win your case. The typical fee ranges from 25% to 40% of the settlement or judgment amount. Always discuss fee structures during your initial consultation.
What should I bring to my initial consultation with a medical malpractice lawyer?
Bring any relevant medical records, documentation of treatments received, correspondence with healthcare providers or insurance companies, and any notes youve taken about your experience and symptoms. This information will help the attorney assess your case more effectively.
