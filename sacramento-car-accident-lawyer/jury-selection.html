Frequently Asked Questions

How does the jury selection process work in a car accident lawsuit?
The jury selection process, known as voir dire, involves questioning potential jurors to assess their impartiality and suitability. Both attorneys can ask questions to identify any biases or conflicts of interest that may affect a jurors ability to fairly decide the case. They can then dismiss certain jurors through peremptory challenges or for cause.
What criteria do lawyers consider when selecting jurors for a car accident case?
Lawyers look for potential biases related to the specifics of the car accident, such as past experiences with similar accidents, relationships with law enforcement or insurance companies, and preconceived notions about liability and damages. They aim to select jurors who can be objective and fair.
Can I influence which jurors are selected for my car accident trial?
While you cannot directly choose the jurors, your lawyer will represent your interests during voir dire by asking strategic questions and using challenges to remove unsuitable candidates. Effective communication with your lawyer about any concerns or preferences is crucial for ensuring an impartial jury.
