Frequently Asked Questions

How does a contingency fee arrangement work in medical malpractice cases?
In a contingency fee arrangement, the lawyer agrees to represent you without an upfront payment. Instead, they receive a percentage of any settlement or judgment awarded if your case is successful. If you lose the case, the lawyer typically does not receive any payment.
What percentage do medical malpractice lawyers usually take as their fee in contingency arrangements?
The percentage can vary but commonly ranges from 25% to 40% of the total recovery amount. Its important to discuss and agree on this percentage with your lawyer before signing any agreement.
Are there any additional costs I might be responsible for in a contingency fee arrangement?
Yes, there may be additional costs such as court filing fees, expert witness fees, and other litigation expenses. Some lawyers may cover these costs upfront and deduct them from your final settlement or judgment, while others may expect you to pay them as they arise. Be sure to clarify this with your lawyer beforehand.
