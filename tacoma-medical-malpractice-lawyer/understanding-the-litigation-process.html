Frequently Asked Questions

What are the initial steps a medical malpractice lawyer takes when assessing a potential case?
A medical malpractice lawyer will first conduct a thorough review of the patients medical records, consult with medical experts to evaluate the standard of care provided, and determine if there is sufficient evidence to support a claim of negligence.
How long do I have to file a medical malpractice lawsuit?
The statute of limitations for filing a medical malpractice lawsuit varies by state but typically ranges from one to three years from the date of injury or discovery of harm. Its crucial to consult with an attorney promptly to ensure your case is filed within the applicable time frame.
What types of damages can be recovered in a medical malpractice case?
In a medical malpractice case, you may be entitled to recover various damages including compensatory damages for medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering; as well as punitive damages if gross negligence or malicious intent is proven.
How does a contingency fee arrangement work in medical malpractice cases?
Under a contingency fee arrangement, you do not pay any upfront legal fees. Instead, your attorney receives a percentage of the settlement or court award if you win your case. If you lose, you typically owe nothing for legal services rendered.
