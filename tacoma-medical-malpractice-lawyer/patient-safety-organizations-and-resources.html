Frequently Asked Questions

What role do Patient Safety Organizations (PSOs) play in medical malpractice cases?
PSOs collect and analyze patient safety data to improve healthcare quality. While they aim to enhance patient safety, the information they gather can sometimes be used by medical malpractice lawyers to understand systemic issues that may have contributed to a clients injury.
How can accessing resources from PSOs benefit a medical malpractice case?
Resources from PSOs, such as reports on common medical errors or best practices for patient safety, can provide insights into standard care protocols. This information helps attorneys establish whether there was a deviation from accepted practices in their client’s case.
Are communications with PSOs protected in legal proceedings involving medical malpractice claims?
Yes, under the Patient Safety and Quality Improvement Act (PSQIA), certain communications with PSOs are privileged and confidential. This means they cannot be disclosed in legal proceedings, which encourages open reporting and discussion about errors without fear of litigation repercussions.
