Frequently Asked Questions

What is informed consent in the context of medical treatment?
Informed consent is the process by which a patient is provided with comprehensive information about their medical condition, treatment options, risks, and benefits, enabling them to make a voluntary and educated decision regarding their care.
How does lack of informed consent relate to medical malpractice?
Lack of informed consent can be grounds for a medical malpractice claim if a patient undergoes a procedure without being fully informed about the risks and alternatives, resulting in harm or injury they were not prepared for.
Can a patient refuse treatment after giving initial consent?
Yes, patients have the right to withdraw their consent at any time before or during treatment. Patient autonomy ensures that they can change their mind based on new information or personal preference.
What role does documentation play in proving informed consent in court?
Documentation such as signed consent forms and detailed notes from discussions between doctor and patient are crucial evidence in court to prove that proper informed consent was obtained prior to proceeding with treatment.
