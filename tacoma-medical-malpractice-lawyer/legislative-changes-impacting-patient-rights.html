Frequently Asked Questions

What recent legislative changes could affect the statute of limitations for filing a medical malpractice claim?
Recent legislative changes may either extend or shorten the statute of limitations for filing a medical malpractice claim. For example, some states have enacted laws that provide additional time for victims to file claims if the injury was not immediately discoverable. It’s crucial to stay updated on state-specific legislation to ensure compliance.
How do new patient privacy laws impact the process of obtaining medical records for a malpractice case?
New patient privacy laws, such as updates to HIPAA regulations, can make it more challenging to access medical records without explicit patient consent. Lawyers must ensure they follow proper legal channels and obtain necessary authorizations while maintaining compliance with these stringent privacy protections.
Are there any new caps on non-economic damages in recent legislation affecting medical malpractice cases?
Yes, some states have recently introduced or adjusted caps on non-economic damages in medical malpractice cases. These caps limit the amount plaintiffs can receive for pain and suffering, emotional distress, and other non-economic harms. Its essential to review current state-specific legislation to understand how these caps may influence potential settlements or verdicts.
