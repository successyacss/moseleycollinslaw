Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifications should a medical malpractice lawyer have?
A qualified medical malpractice lawyer should have a strong background in both law and medicine, including experience handling similar cases, a track record of successful settlements or verdicts, and membership in relevant professional organizations like the American Bar Association or state-specific associations.
How can I determine if a medical malpractice lawyer is experienced?
You can determine their experience by asking about the number of medical malpractice cases they have handled, their success rate, specific outcomes of past cases, and requesting references from past clients. Additionally, look for any published reviews or testimonials.
What fees are associated with hiring a medical malpractice lawyer?
Most medical malpractice lawyers work on a contingency fee basis, meaning they only get paid if you win your case. Typically, the fee ranges from 25% to 40% of the settlement or judgment amount. Its essential to discuss all potential costs upfront during your initial consultation.
