Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifies as medical malpractice in Seattle?
Medical malpractice occurs when a healthcare professional deviates from the standard of care, resulting in injury or harm to the patient. This can include misdiagnosis, surgical errors, medication mistakes, and failure to obtain informed consent.
How long do I have to file a medical malpractice lawsuit in Seattle?
In Washington State, including Seattle, you generally have three years from the date of the alleged malpractice or one year from discovering the injury to file a lawsuit. However, there is an overall statute of repose that bars claims after eight years regardless of discovery.
What should I look for when choosing a medical malpractice lawyer in Seattle?
Look for an attorney with specific experience in medical malpractice cases, a track record of successful outcomes, strong client testimonials, and transparent fee structures. Its also beneficial if they offer free consultations to discuss your case initially.
